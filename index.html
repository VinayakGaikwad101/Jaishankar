Shri S Jaishankar

1955 - Present

Visionary Diplomat

Shri S. Jaishankar, a distinguished Indian diplomat and politician, has made remarkable contributions to India's foreign policy and global engagements. With his extensive expertise and profound understanding of international affairs, Jaishankar has played a pivotal role in strengthening India's relationships with major powers and bolstering its presence on the global stage. Having served as India's Foreign Secretary and Ambassador to the United States and China, his strategic thinking and diplomatic finesse have fostered economic growth, regional stability, and collaborative efforts in addressing global challenges. Jaishankar's pragmatic approach, intellectual acumen, and unwavering commitment to India's national interests have earned him respect worldwide, leaving an indelible mark on India's diplomacy and shaping its future engagements with the world.

Achievements

  • Joined the Indian Foreign Service (IFS) in 1977.
  • Held diplomatic positions in Moscow, Tokyo, and served as India's High Commissioner to Singapore
  • Appointed as Joint Secretary (Americas) at the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) in 2004.
  • Played a crucial role in the India-U.S. civil nuclear agreement in 2008.
  • Appointed as India's Ambassador to China in 2013
  • Became India's Foreign Secretary in January 2015.
  • Prioritized strengthening relationships with neighboring countries and engaging major global powers.
  • Appointed as India's Minister of External Affairs in 2018.
  • Known for expertise, strategic thinking, and commitment to India's national interests.
